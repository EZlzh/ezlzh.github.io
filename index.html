Biography

Zihang Lin is a Visiting Predoctoral Fellow in the Center for Science of Science and Innovation (CSSI) at the Kellogg School of Management at Northwestern University. He is also an undergraduate in the School of Computer Science at Fudan University in China. He is going to apply to PhD programs in Fall 2023.

His research interests include network science, computational social science, and science of science. He is engaged in large-scale scientific data processing and analysis of multiple databases, trying to attain a deeper understanding of science and innovation.

Research

SciNet: A large-scale open data lake for the science of science
We are building a large-scale open data lake to better facilitate large-scale data-driven research in the science of science.
This work is advised by Prof. Dashun Wang and Dr. Yian Yin (expected).
Empirical Analysis of Structural Hole Spanner Detection Methodologies in Coauthorship Networks
We are analyzing the academic performance and cooperative relationship between different types of scholars, such as structural hole scholars and opinion leaders.
This work is co-advised by Prof. Yang Chen and Dr. Qingyuan Gong.
Review on Structural Hole Theory in Social Network Analysis
A comprehensive review on the foundation, detection and practical application of structural hole theory, facilitating researchers and developers to better apply structural hole theory and derive value-added tools.
The paper has been accepted by IEEE Transactions on Computational Social Systems (TCSS), and is co-advised by Prof. Yang Chen, Dr. Qingyuan Gong, Prof. Aaron Yi Ding, Prof. Atte Oksanen.
